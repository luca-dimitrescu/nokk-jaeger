Vad är katter?

Katten (Felis catus), även kallad huskatt eller huskatt, är ett litet domesticerat köttätande däggdjur. Det är den enda domesticerade arten i familjen Felidae. Framsteg inom arkeologi och genetik har visat att domesticeringen av katten skedde i Främre Orienten omkring 7500 f.Kr.

Vanligt namn: Katt

Vetenskapligt namn: Felis catus

Typ: Däggdjur

Diet: Rovdjur

Genomsnittlig storlek: 71cm

Medelvikt: 2-9kg

Sphynx


The Sphynx cat is a unique and distinctive breed known for its hairless appearance and striking features. Originating in the 1960s, the Sphynx cat's lack of fur is due to a natural genetic mutation, making their skin feel warm and suede-like to the touch.

Despite their lack of a furry coat, Sphynx cats come in a variety of skin colors and patterns, often showcasing intriguing pigmentation. They are known for their friendly and affectionate nature, often seeking human companionship and warmth. Their playful and curious disposition makes them excellent companions.

Due to their hairlessness, Sphynx cats require regular grooming to remove skin oils and maintain cleanliness, making them a unique and special addition to any household.

The price of a Sphynx varies between $2,100 - $11,000​

  • Sphynx cats are prone to certain health issues like hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) and skin conditions due to their lack of fur.

  • They are more sensitive to temperature changes and require protection from extreme cold or heat.

  • Due to their higher metabolism and lack of fur, they may need a higher calorie diet to maintain their body weight.

Abyssinian


The Abyssinian cat is one of the oldest and most cherished breeds in the world, known for its striking resemblance to the ancient cats depicted in Egyptian art. Characterized by its slender, muscular body, large ears, and expressive almond
-shaped eyes, the Abyssinian is an elegant and graceful cat. Its coat is short and dense, with a unique ticked pattern that gives it a wild, almost jungle-like appearance.

Abyssinians are highly active and playful, often described as curious and intelligent. They thrive on interaction and enjoy being involved in their owners' activities, making them affectionate and engaging companions. Their lively nature and affectionate demeanor make them a favorite among cat enthusiasts

The price of an Abyssinian varies between $500 - $1,500​

  • The Abyssinian is believed to be one of the oldest cat breeds and may have ancient Egyptian roots, though the exact origin is debated.

  • Their short coat requires minimal grooming, as it is self-cleaning and does not mat easily.

  • They are known for their love of climbing and may require vertical spaces or cat trees to satisfy their instinctive climbing behavior.

Chartreux

The Chartreux cat is a rare and ancient breed known for its stunning blue-gray coat and captivating copper or gold eyes. Originating from France, this breed has a rich history that dates back to medieval times. The Chartreux is known for its robust and muscular build, yet it moves with a graceful and agile elegance. Their dense, water-resistant fur is often described as woolly to the touch, providing them with a unique and plush appearance.

Chartreux cats are renowned for their gentle and affectionate nature. They are quiet, often communicating through soft chirps and purrs rather than loud meows. Despite their calm demeanor, they are playful and enjoy interactive toys and games. Loyal and devoted to their owners, Chartreux cats form strong bonds and are often seen following their human companions around the house. Their combination of striking looks and endearing personality makes the Chartreux a beloved breed among cat lovers.

The price of a Chartreux varies between $1,500 - $2,000​

  • Chartreux cats have a genetic predisposition to certain eye conditions, such as strabismus (crossed eyes).

  • They are relatively quiet cats compared to some other breeds, often using soft sounds rather than loud meows.

  • Chartreux cats can adapt well to various living environments, from apartments to larger homes.

Siberian

The Siberian cat is a robust and striking breed, notable for its long, dense coat and powerful physique. Originating from Russia, this breed is well-adapted to cold climates, with a thick, water-resistant fur that feels soft and plush. Siberians have a broad, muscular body and a bushy tail, which adds to their distinctive appearance.

Known for their gentle and friendly nature, Siberians are affectionate with their families and tend to get along well with children and other pets. They are playful and curious, often engaging in interactive games and exploring their surroundings. Despite their large size, Siberians are agile and enjoy an active lifestyle. Their intelligence and loyalty make them wonderful companions, often forming strong bonds with their owners.

The price of a Siberian varies between $1,000 - $2,000​

  • Siberians are considered to be hypoallergenic because they produce fewer allergenic proteins compared to other breeds

  • Their dense coat is not only warm but also water-resistant, helping them stay dry in wet conditions.

  • Siberians enjoy interactive play and need regular exercise to maintain their health and happiness.

Savannah

The Savannah cat is an exotic and striking breed, a hybrid of a domestic cat and a serval, a wild African cat. This breed is known for its tall, lean body, large ears, and distinctive spotted coat, giving it a wild and elegant appearance. Savannahs are one of the largest domestic cat breeds, with a strong and muscular build.

Savannah cats are highly active and intelligent, often displaying dog-like behaviors such as fetching and walking on a leash. They are very social and form strong bonds with their owners, enjoying interaction and playtime. Their high energy levels and curiosity make them adventurous and sometimes mischievous. Despite their wild ancestry, Savannahs are affectionate and loyal, making them unique and engaging companions.

The price of a Savannah varies between $1,000 - $20,000​​

  • Due to their hybrid nature, ownership of Savannah cats can be restricted or regulated in some areas.

  • Savannah cats come in different sizes depending on their generation, with F1 (first-generation) being the largest.

  • Early socialization is crucial for Savannahs to ensure they develop well-rounded and friendly personalities.

Ragdoll

The Ragdoll cat is a large, affectionate breed known for its striking blue eyes and semi-long, silky coat. This breed gets its name from its tendency to go limp and relaxed when picked up, much like a ragdoll. Ragdolls have a sturdy build with soft, plush fur that comes in various color patterns, often with distinctive points on their ears, face, paws, and tail.

Ragdolls are renowned for their gentle and calm demeanor. They are highly sociable and enjoy being around people, often following their owners from room to room. Their affectionate nature makes them excellent lap cats and they typically get along well with children and other pets. Despite their relaxed temperament, Ragdolls are playful and enjoy interactive toys and games. Their combination of beauty, docility, and friendly personality makes Ragdolls a beloved breed among cat enthusiasts.

The price of a Ragdoll varies between $800 - $2,500​

  • All Ragdolls are born with blue eyes, but as they age, the shade of blue may deepen.

  • Ragdolls are sensitive to temperature changes, and their coat is designed to keep them cool rather than warm.

  • Ragdolls are prone to certain health issues like heart disease and kidney problems, so regular vet check-ups are important.

Russian Blue

The Russian Blue is a graceful and elegant breed, known for its short, dense coat of bluish-gray fur and striking green eyes. This breed has a slender, muscular body and a refined appearance, exuding both beauty and sophistication.

Russian Blues are known for their gentle and reserved nature. They are affectionate with their families but tend to be shy around strangers. Intelligent and playful, they enjoy interactive toys and games, making them engaging companions. Despite their playful side, Russian Blues appreciate a calm and quiet environment, often finding a favorite spot to relax. Their loyal and loving demeanor makes them wonderful pets for those seeking a devoted and serene feline friend.

The price of a Russian Blue varies between $400 - $3,000​

  • Their coat requires regular brushing to maintain its soft texture and to reduce shedding.

  • Russian Blues are known for being somewhat reserved, but they are incredibly loyal and loving once they form a bond.

  • They are intelligent and can be trained to perform tricks or use a litter box with ease.

Nowregian Forest Cat

The Norwegian Forest Cat is a majestic and sturdy breed, known for its thick, water-resistant coat and tufted ears. Originating from the forests of Norway, this breed has a large, muscular build and a bushy tail, giving it a wild yet elegant appearance.

Norwegian Forest Cats are friendly and sociable, forming strong bonds with their families. They are playful and curious, often enjoying climbing and exploring their surroundings. Despite their adventurous nature, they are also calm and affectionate, making them wonderful companions. Their beautiful appearance, combined with their gentle and loving personality, makes the Norwegian Forest Cat a beloved breed among cat enthusiasts.

The price of a Norwegian Forest Cat varies between $600 - $1,200

  • They are excellent hunters and have strong prey drives, which is an instinctive trait from their wild ancestors.

  • Their thick coat requires regular grooming to prevent matting and to manage shedding.

  • They are well-adapted to cold climates with their thick fur and bushy tail, making them resilient in harsh weather conditions.

Scottish Fold

The Scottish Fold is a unique and endearing breed, easily recognized by its distinctive folded ears, which give it an owl-like appearance. This breed has a rounded head, large round eyes, and a sturdy, medium-sized body covered in a soft, dense coat that comes in various colors and patterns.

Scottish Folds are known for their affectionate and gentle nature. They are friendly and enjoy being around people, often forming strong bonds with their families. Playful and curious, they love interactive toys and games but are also content to relax and snuggle with their owners. Their calm and easygoing demeanor makes them excellent companions for households with children or other pets. The combination of their unique appearance and loving personality makes the Scottish Fold a favorite among cat lovers.

The price of a Scottish Fold varies between $1,000 - $3,000​

  • The gene responsible for their folded ears can also lead to cartilage problems and other skeletal issues.

  • Their folded ears may require regular cleaning to prevent infections.

  • Scottish Folds are generally adaptable and can adjust well to different living environments and routines.

Persian

The Persian cat is a luxurious and elegant breed, known for its long, flowing coat and distinctive flat-faced appearance. This breed has a round face, large expressive eyes, and a sturdy, medium to large-sized body. Their plush fur comes in a wide variety of colors and patterns, adding to their regal look. .

Persians are renowned for their calm and gentle temperament. They are affectionate and enjoy quiet, relaxed environments, often seeking out cozy spots to lounge. While they are generally low-energy, Persians appreciate attention and gentle playtime with their owners. Their laid-back nature makes them well-suited for indoor living and they thrive in loving, peaceful households. Regular grooming is essential to maintain their beautiful coat, but their sweet and affectionate personality makes the effort worthwhile. Persians are cherished for their serene presence and loving companionship.

The price of a Persian varies between $500 - $5,000​

  • The flat face of Persians can lead to breathing difficulties and other respiratory problems, known as brachycephalic airway syndrome.

  • Their large, prominent eyes may be prone to tearing and discharge, requiring regular cleaning.

  • Persians may need a special diet to manage their weight and maintain their coat health.

Manx

The Manx cat is a distinctive breed known for its unique lack of a tail, a result of a natural genetic mutation. This breed has a rounded, sturdy body with strong hind legs that are slightly longer than the front legs, giving it a characteristic hopping gait. The Manx's dense double coat can be short or long and comes in a variety of colors and patterns .

Manx cats are known for their friendly and playful nature. They are intelligent and enjoy interactive toys and games, often showing a dog-like willingness to fetch and follow their owners around. Despite their playful side, Manx cats are also affectionate and form strong bonds with their families, enjoying cuddle time and being close to their human companions. Their social and adaptable temperament makes them great pets for families with children and other animals. The combination of their unique appearance and loving personality makes the Manx a cherished breed.

The price of a Manx varies between $500 - $800​

  • The Manx gene can sometimes lead to spinal problems, such as a shortened spinal cord, which can cause mobility issues.

  • There are different tail lengths in Manx cats, ranging from no tail at all to a short stub, which can affect their balance.

  • Their coat can require regular grooming, especially in long-haired varieties, to prevent tangles and matting.

Aegean

The Aegean cat is a natural breed originating from the Greek Cycladic Islands, known for its lively and friendly personality. This breed typically has a semi-longhaired coat that is often white with patches of color, such as black, blue, red, or cream. Aegean cats have a medium-sized, muscular build and striking almond-shaped eyes, usually green or blue. .

Aegean cats are known for their playful and sociable nature. They are highly active and enjoy interactive play, often engaging in hunting-like activities. These cats are also very affectionate and form strong bonds with their families, enjoying both human companionship and attention. Their friendly demeanor makes them great pets for households with children and other pets. Adaptable and intelligent, Aegean cats thrive in environments where they can explore and interact with their surroundings. The combination of their striking appearance and loving personality makes the Aegean cat a delightful companion.

The price of an Aegean varies between $300 - $600​

  • Aegean cats are known for enjoying the outdoors and may prefer having access to a secure outdoor space.

  • They tend to have a longer lifespan compared to some other breeds, often living into their teens.

  • Aegeans are generally healthy cats with few hereditary health issues, making them a low-maintenance breed in terms of health concerns

Brittish Shorthair

The British Shorthair is a popular and well-loved breed, known for its round face, dense plush coat, and large, round eyes that can be copper, blue, or green. This breed has a stocky, muscular build and a calm, dignified demeanor.

British Shorthairs are known for their easygoing and affectionate nature. They are friendly and enjoy the company of their families but are not overly demanding of attention. These cats are typically quiet and patient, making them excellent companions for households with children and other pets. While they are not as active as some other breeds, they do enjoy playing and interacting with their owners.

Their thick, dense coat requires regular grooming to keep it in good condition, but their overall care is relatively low-maintenance. British Shorthairs are prized for their combination of a charming appearance and a gentle, loving personality, making them a beloved breed among cat enthusiasts.

The price of a British Shorthair varies between $800 - $2,000​

  • British Shorthairs come in various sizes, but they generally have a sturdy, stocky build.

  • Their dense coat requires regular brushing to prevent matting and maintain its plush appearance.

  • While they are known for their calm demeanor, British Shorthairs enjoy interactive play and can be quite lively when engaged.

Birman

The Birman cat is a striking and elegant breed known for its silky, medium to long coat, deep blue eyes, and distinctive color-point pattern with pure white "gloves" on its paws. This breed has a sturdy, muscular build with a gentle and affectionate demeanor.

Birmans are known for their friendly and social nature. They are affectionate and enjoy spending time with their families, often seeking out attention and companionship. Birmans are playful and curious, making them engaging companions who enjoy interactive toys and games. Despite their playful side, they are also known for their calm and gentle temperament, making them great pets for households with children and other animals.

Their beautiful coat requires regular grooming to keep it looking its best, but Birmans generally enjoy the grooming process and the attention that comes with it. Their combination of stunning appearance and loving personality makes the Birman a cherished breed among cat lovers.

The price of a Birman varies between $700 - $1,000​

  • Birmans have a semi-long coat that is less prone to matting than other long-haired breeds, but they still require regular grooming to keep their fur in good condition and to prevent tangles.

  • Birmans are generally healthy, but they can be prone to certain genetic conditions such as hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) and kidney disease. Regular veterinary check-ups are important to monitor their health.

  • Birmans are known for being relatively quiet compared to other breeds. They typically have soft, gentle voices and are not overly vocal, making them a good choice for people who prefer a quieter pet.
